About Spark

Spark is a collection of visualizations for Fitbit physical activity data. The purpose is to explore fun, engaging, and creative ways of visualizing exercise. Spark uses different metaphors to represent different features of physical exercise, such as time, step count, and elevation (planned).

With Spark, you can:

  • ✼ See your Fitbit data visualized live
  • ✼ Interact with the visualizations (soon)
  • ✼ View Sparks over a week, month, or year (planned)

I would love to hear your feedback on features and visualizations!

Story

Spark was based on one of eight concept ideas I had for my PhD research on technology that motivates older adults to exercise: using exercise to dynamically generate artwork that is displayed in the home. Given my past work on interactive art and my newfound interest in data visualization, I wasted no time implementing Spark, starting December 1, 2011.

Colophon

This site is designed using Twitter Bootstrap. The front-end is written in JQuery, with the data visualizations created in Raphaël, HTML5 Canvas, and HighCharts. The back-end is in Python, Flask, and Jinja2 on Google App Engine.
